Services

Each service is mapped directly to a mandatory obligation under Kenya's CII Regulations (Legal Notice 44 of 2024). The regulation reference is shown on every card.

Service 01

CII Risk Assessment & Risk Register

Reg 17 — Annual Risk Assessment Reg 31(2)(j) — Baseline Security Reg 31(2)(l) — Risk Register

The annual cybersecurity risk assessment is mandatory for all designated CII owners. Most operators are already in breach — the 12-month deadline from commencement (9 February 2024) fell in February 2025.

We deliver a structured assessment that identifies vulnerabilities your CII is exposed to, documents them in a live risk register, and provides a prioritised remediation roadmap. The output is designed to satisfy NC4 compliance reporting requirements.

  • Threat identification and scenario modelling
  • Vulnerability assessment across OT/ICS assets
  • Consequence and likelihood analysis
  • Risk register creation and maintenance
  • Remediation prioritisation and roadmap
  • NC4-ready compliance documentation
Regulatory obligation The Director may direct designated CII owners to conduct annual risk assessments under Regulation 20. Failure to comply is an offence under the Act.
Service 02

IEC 62443 Gap Assessment

Reg 31 — Baseline Security Reg 71(3) — Best Practice Standards Reg 31(2)(a) — Asset Classification

Regulation 71(3) explicitly permits CII owners to identify and adopt global best practices on their own initiative. IEC 62443 is the only international standard purpose-built for industrial control system and OT security — and the natural technical anchor for energy, water, and transport CII operators.

We hold all four ISA/IEC 62443 certificates (Fundamentals, Risk Assessment, Design, Maintenance) — the Expert-level certification stack, not entry-level. Our gap assessments map your OT environment against the standard and produce an actionable remediation programme.

  • OT/ICS asset identification, classification and cataloguing
  • Zone and conduit modelling
  • Target Security Level (SL-T) assessment
  • Security Level verification across Foundational Requirements
  • Prioritised gap report and remediation roadmap
  • Vendor and supplier security requirement development

Service 03

CII Compliance Audit

Reg 46 — Compliance Report Reg 47–50 — Audit Requirements Reg 31(2)(k) — Annual Internal Audit

Regulation 31(2)(k) requires CII owners to conduct annual internal cybersecurity audits to check compliance with the directives. Regulation 46 defines what a compliance report must contain. NC4 auditors follow Form CMCA 6 — a structured template covering network security, system security, data security, application security, and physical security.

We conduct pre-audit readiness assessments structured against the CMCA 6 template, identify gaps before NC4 auditors do, and help you produce a compliant compliance report.

  • Pre-audit readiness assessment (CMCA 6 structure)
  • Network security review: firewall, IDPS, VLAN segmentation
  • System security: patching, endpoint, access controls
  • Data security: classification, encryption, backup and recovery
  • Physical security assessment
  • Compliance report drafting (Reg 46)
Service 04

OT Penetration Testing

Reg 39(d) — Security Audits & Pentest Reg 39(c) — Intrusion Detection Reg 29(2)(b) — Access Limitation

Regulation 39(d) explicitly requires CII owners to adopt procedures for conducting regular security audits and penetration testing to identify vulnerabilities and system weaknesses. This is not optional — it is a named mandatory measure for virtual access to CII.

We hold OSCP and OSEP (Offensive Security Experienced Penetration Tester) certifications. Our OT penetration tests are designed for operational environments — testing without disrupting industrial processes.

  • OT/ICS network penetration testing
  • Active Directory and IT/OT boundary assessment
  • SCADA and HMI access path testing
  • Remote access and VPN security assessment
  • Social engineering and phishing simulation
  • Remediation guidance and re-test
Suitable for: Energy (SCADA/EMS), Water (SCADA), Transport (signalling and control), Financial (critical systems)
Service 05

CISO Advisory & Designation Support

Reg 32 — CISO Designation Reg 33 — CISO Qualifications Reg 34 — Mandatory Policies Reg 30 — Awareness Programme

Every designated CII owner must appoint a Chief Information Security Officer. The qualification requirements are specific: Kenyan citizen, master's degree in information security or related field, at least five years demonstrable professional experience in CII protection, and Chapter Six of the Constitution compliance.

Most organisations do not have a person who meets this specification — particularly the CII-specific experience requirement. We help design the CISO function, build the policies and procedures the CISO is responsible for, and provide ongoing technical advisory support behind a locally-credentialed appointment.

  • CISO role design and job specification
  • Internal cybersecurity policy development (Reg 34)
  • Organisational cybersecurity procedures and codes of practice
  • Cybersecurity awareness programme design (Reg 30)
  • Staff training: IR, insider threats, risk assessment, emerging tech
  • Ongoing CISO advisory and technical support
Policy deadline The 6-month deadline to formulate organisational policies, procedures and codes of practice (Regulation 34) was August 2024. Operators who have not completed this are already in breach.
Service 06

Incident Response Planning & Preparedness

Reg 65 — Incident Reporting (24 hrs) Reg 20 — IR Plan Directive Reg 31(2)(m) — Exercises & Drills Reg 31(2)(f) — Security Monitoring

CII owners must report all cybersecurity incidents to the relevant Sectoral Cybersecurity Operations Centre within 24 hours of becoming aware. Without a tested incident response plan, documented escalation paths, and a reporting process, most organisations cannot meet this obligation.

The regulations also require participation in cybersecurity exercises and drills with the Committee (Reg 31(2)(m)) — and security monitoring and response processes for timely detection of incidents (Reg 31(2)(f)).

  • Incident response plan development
  • 24-hour reporting procedures and Sectoral COC coordination
  • Escalation paths and stakeholder communication plans
  • Security monitoring and detection capability assessment
  • Tabletop exercises and cyber drills
  • Post-incident review and lessons-learned process
Service 07

Business Continuity & Disaster Recovery

Reg 42 — Disaster Recovery Site Reg 31(2)(i) — BC/DR Plan Reg 28 — Data Localisation

Regulation 42 requires CII owners to establish a disaster recovery and backup site that is geographically separate from the primary CII location. The backup site must be capable of restoring operations in the event of a compromise or destruction of the primary infrastructure.

Regulation 31(2)(i) further requires periodic testing and updating of business continuity and disaster recovery plans. Regulation 28 requires that CII data remains in Kenya — any offshore storage requires Committee approval.

  • Business continuity plan development and review
  • Disaster recovery site requirements assessment
  • Backup procedures and RTO/RPO target setting
  • Data localisation compliance review (Reg 28)
  • BC/DR plan testing and tabletop exercises
  • Restoration process documentation and training
